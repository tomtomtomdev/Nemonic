# Wyckoff 2.0 Trading Skill
## Based on: *Wyckoff 2.0* by Rubén Villahermosa

---

## CORE PHILOSOPHY

The market is moved by a **Composite Man (CM)** — the collective force of large
institutional traders, market makers, and "bandar" (in IDX context). The CM operates
in cycles: **accumulate → markup → distribute → markdown**. Your job is to identify
which phase the CM is currently in, and trade in the same direction.

> "Price tells us what is happening. Volume tells us why."

---

## THE FOUR MARKET PHASES

```
ACCUMULATION → MARKUP → DISTRIBUTION → MARKDOWN
     ↑                                      |
     └──────────────── (repeat) ────────────┘
```

| Phase        | CM Action       | Volume Pattern       | Trader Action     |
|--------------|-----------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Accumulation | Absorbing supply | High on down-bars → diminishing | Prepare to buy |
| Markup       | Pushing price up | Volume confirms advance | Hold / add on pullbacks |
| Distribution | Offloading stock | High on up-bars → stalling | Prepare to sell |
| Markdown     | Pushing price down | Volume confirms decline | Stay out / short |

## WYCKOFF EVENTS (KEY LABELS)

### In ACCUMULATION Schematics

| Event | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| **PS** – Preliminary Support | First attempt to stop the decline; big buyers appear |
| **SC** – Selling Climax | Panic selling, high volume, wide spread down-bar; bottom candidate |
| **AR** – Automatic Rally | Price bounces after SC; defines top of trading range |
| **ST** – Secondary Test | Price retests SC area on lower volume = sign of strength |
| **Spring** | Price breaks below SC support briefly, traps sellers, reverses — the **best entry point** |
| **Test of Spring** | Low-volume revisit of Spring level; confirms demand absorbed supply |
| **SOS** – Sign of Strength | Strong advance on high volume breaking above trading range |
| **LPS** – Last Point of Support | Pullback after SOS on low volume = buy opportunity before markup |
| **BU/BUEC** – Back Up | Throwback to broken resistance (now support) before markup continues |

### In DISTRIBUTION Schematics

| Event | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| **PSY** – Preliminary Supply | First signs of selling after uptrend |
| **BC** – Buying Climax | Euphoric buying, wide spread up-bar; top candidate |
| **AR** – Automatic Reaction | Price falls after BC; defines bottom of trading range |
| **ST** – Secondary Test | Retests BC area on lower volume = sign of weakness |
| **UTAD** – Upthrust After Distribution | False breakout above trading range; traps buyers — the **best short entry** |
| **SOW** – Sign of Weakness | Sharp decline on high volume, breaking below trading range |
| **LPSY** – Last Point of Supply | Weak rally after SOW on low volume = sell/short opportunity |

## THE FIVE PHASES (A → E)

### Accumulation
- **Phase A**: Trend stopping (PS, SC, AR, ST)
- **Phase B**: Building the cause — ranging, volatility decreasing
- **Phase C**: The test — Spring or shakeout (most important phase)
- **Phase D**: Demand winning — SOS and LPS sequence, price trending up within range
- **Phase E**: Markup — price leaves the range, trend begins

### Distribution
- **Phase A**: Trend stopping (PSY, BC, AR, ST)
- **Phase B**: Building the cause — ranging, absorbing demand
- **Phase C**: The test — UTAD or Upthrust
- **Phase D**: Supply winning — SOW and LPSY sequence
- **Phase E**: Markdown — price leaves range, downtrend begins

## VOLUME ANALYSIS RULES (Effort vs. Result)

These are non-negotiable principles. Always check:

| Scenario | Interpretation |
|----------|----------------|
| Wide spread UP + High volume | Genuine demand; bullish |
| Wide spread UP + Low volume | No institutional support; suspicious; potential trap |
| Wide spread DOWN + High volume | Genuine supply; bearish |
| Narrow spread + High volume | Absorption — CM absorbing the other side; reversal likely |
| Narrow spread + Low volume | No interest; trend may continue without opposition |
| **No Demand bar** | Up-bar, narrow spread, low volume after upswing = weakness |
| **No Supply bar** | Down-bar, narrow spread, low volume after decline = strength |

> **Rule**: If price moves a lot (result) on low volume (effort), be skeptical.
> If high volume produces little price movement, someone is absorbing — this is KEY.

## VOLUME PROFILE INTEGRATION (Wyckoff 2.0 Addition)

Volume Profile adds WHERE volume traded, not just how much.

### Key Levels

| Term | Meaning | Trading Use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **POC** – Point of Control | Price with most volume traded | Strong magnet; expect price to revisit |
| **VAH** – Value Area High | Top of 70% value zone | Resistance in uptrends |
| **VAL** – Value Area Low | Bottom of 70% value zone | Support in downtrends |
| **HVN** – High Volume Node | Price accepted here; consensus | Expect consolidation, support/resistance |
| **LVN** – Low Volume Node | Price rejected here; no consensus | Price moves fast through these zones |
| **Volume Gap** | Large LVN / air pocket | Price targets the next HVN rapidly |

### How to Combine with Wyckoff

- **Spring at VAL** = very high-probability buy setup
- **UTAD at VAH** = very high-probability sell/short setup
- **LPS at POC** = strong confirmation of demand
- **SOW through LVN** = fast move expected downward
- **SOS through LVN** = fast move expected upward
- **Price stuck in HVN after SOS** = distribution may be starting; caution

## COMPOSITE MAN / BANDAR LOGIC (IDX Context)

On IDX, broker codes are disclosed. Use this framework to track the CM:

1. **Accumulation phase**: Look for 1-2 dominant broker codes consistently on the buy side (net buyer), especially on weakness bars. In Stockbit Bandarmology / Broker Flow, these brokers accumulate quietly.

2. **Markup**: The dominant broker reduces size; retail starts chasing. Volume increases but CM is distributing early longs.

3. **Distribution**: Same dominant brokers now appear on the sell side, often through different sub-codes. High volume on up-bars but price stalls.

4. **Spring logic**: CM deliberately pushes price below visible support to trigger retail stop-losses, buys at discount, then reverses. On IDX: look for sudden high-volume down-bar followed immediately by strong recovery same day.

5. **UTAD logic**: CM pushes above resistance to trigger retail breakout buyers, sells into their demand, then reverses sharply.

---

## CAUSE AND EFFECT (Wyckoff's Count)

The longer and deeper the accumulation, the bigger the subsequent markup.

- **Wide, long trading range** = big markup coming
- **Narrow, shallow trading range** = small move
- Use Point & Figure count if precise targeting needed
- On bar charts: estimate height of range × 3 as rough target for markup

## STEP-BY-STEP ANALYSIS WORKFLOW

When analyzing any stock/chart, follow this sequence:

```
STEP 1 — IDENTIFY THE TREND (weekly/monthly context)
  └─ Is price in uptrend, downtrend, or sideways?

STEP 2 — IDENTIFY THE PHASE
  └─ Accumulation? Distribution? Markup? Markdown?
  └─ What phase (A/B/C/D/E) are we in?

STEP 3 — LABEL KEY EVENTS
  └─ Mark SC/BC, AR, ST, Spring/UTAD, SOS/SOW, LPS/LPSY

STEP 4 — APPLY VOLUME ANALYSIS
  └─ Check effort vs result on every key bar
  └─ Look for absorption, No Demand, No Supply

STEP 5 — ADD VOLUME PROFILE
  └─ Mark POC, VAH, VAL, HVN, LVN on current range
  └─ Do Wyckoff events align with VP levels?

STEP 6 — PLAN THE TRADE
  └─ Entry: Spring/LPS (long) or UTAD/LPSY (short)
  └─ Stop: Below Spring low / above UTAD high
  └─ Target: Next HVN or opposite end of value area
  └─ Risk/Reward: Minimum 1:2; prefer 1:3+
```

## HIGH-PROBABILITY SETUPS (Ready-to-Trade)

### Setup 1: Spring + Test (Long)
- **Context**: Accumulation Phase C
- **Entry**: Close of Test bar (narrow spread, low volume, holds above Spring low)
- **Stop**: Below Spring low (with small buffer)
- **Target**: Top of trading range (AR high) → then SOS extension

### Setup 2: LPS After SOS (Long)
- **Context**: Accumulation Phase D
- **Entry**: LPS bar forms (low-volume pullback after SOS)
- **Stop**: Below LPS low
- **Target**: Projected markup = range height above breakout

### Setup 3: UTAD / Upthrust (Short)
- **Context**: Distribution Phase C
- **Entry**: Bar that closes back inside range after UTAD
- **Stop**: Above UTAD high
- **Target**: Bottom of trading range (AR low) → then SOW extension

### Setup 4: LPSY (Short)
- **Context**: Distribution Phase D
- **Entry**: Weak rally bar on low volume after SOW
- **Stop**: Above LPSY high
- **Target**: Projected markdown = range depth below breakdown

## WHAT TO AVOID

- ❌ **Don't buy breakouts blindly** — many are UTADs (traps)
- ❌ **Don't sell breakdowns blindly** — many are Springs (traps)
- ❌ **Don't ignore volume** — price without volume context is incomplete
- ❌ **Don't trade Phase B** — it's noise; wait for Phase C test
- ❌ **Don't force labels** — if the chart doesn't fit, it doesn't fit
- ❌ **Don't trade illiquid stocks with this method** — CM needs scale to operate; on IDX, stick to LQ45/IDX80

## QUICK REFERENCE: MARKET STRENGTH vs WEAKNESS SIGNS

| Bullish (Strength) | Bearish (Weakness) |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| SC on high volume | BC on high volume |
| ST on low volume | ST on low volume (distribution) |
| Spring reversal | UTAD reversal |
| SOS breaks resistance | SOW breaks support |
| LPS on low volume | LPSY on low volume |
| Demand bar (wide up, high vol) | Supply bar (wide down, high vol) |
| No Supply on pullback | No Demand on rally |
| Price holds above POC | Price fails below POC |
| LVN crossed upward fast | LVN crossed downward fast |
| HVN acts as support | HVN acts as resistance |

- Use broker flow to **confirm Wyckoff phase** — accumulation = net buy by 1-2 large brokers
- **LQ45 and IDX80** are ideal universes — sufficient institutional presence for Wyckoff to work
